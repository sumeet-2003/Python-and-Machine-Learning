Rice Crop Diseases Information

Hispa

Causes: Hispa is a common pest in rice crops. The larvae and adults feed on the leaves, causing characteristic shot-hole damage.

Prevention Measures: Use insecticides approved for rice crops. Implement cultural practices such as maintaining proper field hygiene and planting resistant rice varieties.

Brown Spot

Causes: Brown spot is a fungal disease caused by the pathogen Bipolaris oryzae. It thrives in warm and humid conditions, often appearing during periods of high rainfall.

Prevention Measures: Use fungicides recommended for controlling brown spot. Ensure proper drainage and avoid waterlogging in the fields. Practice crop rotation and maintain proper field hygiene.

Leaf Blast

Causes: Leaf blast is a fungal disease caused by the pathogen Pyricularia oryzae. It affects leaves, leaf sheaths, nodes, and panicles, leading to significant yield losses.

Prevention Measures: Use resistant rice varieties if available. Apply fungicides preventively before symptoms appear. Maintain proper field drainage and avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization.
